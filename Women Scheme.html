The Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana-(PMUY)

The Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) is an initiative launched by the Government of India in 2016 with the aim of providing clean cooking fuel to rural and underserved households across the country.
It focuses on slum redevelopment, credit-linked subsidies, affordable rental housing, and women empowerment.
Targeting women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) families, the scheme provides free LPG connections to ensure a smoke-free and healthier environment. This initiative not only addresses health concerns
With a goal of inclusive development, PMUY caters to the housing needs of economically weaker sections, lower-income groups, and middle-income groups.
PMUY contributes significantly to the welfare of women, the improvement of rural health standards, and the preservation of the environment.

Eligibility Criteria :

For : The applicant’s household should not already have an LPG connection in any member's name.
Gender : Female
State :: All Urban Areas
Caste : For all
Annual Income :EWS - upto ₹3,00,000
                            LIG - between ₹3,00,000 - ₹6,00,000
                            MIG 1 - between ₹6,00,000 - ₹12,00,000
                            MIG 2 - between ₹12,00,000 - ₹18,00,000

Required Documents:

  • Proof of Identity: Aadhaar card, Voter ID card, PAN card
  • Proof of Residence: Aadhaar card, Voter ID card, utility bills (electricity/water)
  • Bank Account Details
  • Passport Size Photo
  • Income Certificate
  • Caste Certificate
  • Application Form
  • Property Documents: If already owning a property, relevant documents like property registration papers, ownership proof, etc.

    • Mudra Yojana

    Under this scheme, the Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency (MUDRA) Bank offers loans up to ₹10 lakhs to non-corporate, non-farm small/micro enterprises.

    • The Working Women Hostel Scheme

    The Working Women Hostel Scheme is an initiative by the Government of India aimed at providing safe and affordable accommodation for working women, along with day care facilities for their children
    Its objectives include addressing the women, empowering them, and ensuring social security.
    The scheme provides grants for the construction of new hostels and the expansion of existing ones. These hostels offer essential amenities such as furnished rooms, a common dining hall, recreational facilities, security services.
    By ensuring a safe living environment, the Working Women Hostel Scheme helps women focus on their professional and educational pursuits without worrying about their accommodation, thereby contributing to their overall well-being and advancement.
    By promoting the Hostel for women, the scheme contributes to infrastructure development, creating a positive impact on the lives of women and their families.

    • Women Helpline Scheme

    The Women Helpline Scheme is an initiative by the Government of India designed to provide 24/7 emergency and non-emergency support services to women facing violence or in distress. .
    Launched under the Ministry of Women and Child Development, this scheme aims to offer immediate assistance and information to women affected by various forms of violence, including domestic violence, sexual harassment, trafficking, and more.

    The helpline number, 181, is toll-free and accessible across the country. It provides a range of services, including counseling, rescue, and relice, medical facilities.
    The helpline also coordinates with other women-centric schemes and services, such as One Stop Centers and shelter homes, to ensure comprehensive support and rehabilitation for women in need.
    The Women Helpline Scheme empowers women to seek help and take necessary actions against violence and abuse

    Eligibility Criteria :

    For :Every women in the country
    Gender : Female
    State :All the states
    Cast : For all
    Annual Income : ₹0

    Required Documents:

  • Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, etc
  • Address proof such as ration card, electricity bill, etc
  • Land documents to prove that the applicant does not own a house or plot
  • Certificate from the Sarpanch of the native village stating that the applicant has not previously taken advantage of the Sardar Patel Awas Yojana and meets the B.P.L criteria
  • Income certificates
